# Golden Age of Hip Hop

Mid-1980s to mid-1990s: the era when, per Rolling Stone, "it seemed that every new single reinvented the genre." Marked by diversity, experimentation, Afrocentrism, and political militancy — with strong jazz influence and eclectic [[Sampling in Hip Hop]].

Key artists: Public Enemy, Eric B. & Rakim, KRS-One, De La Soul, A Tribe Called Quest, Gang Starr, Big Daddy Kane. The Native Tongues collective pushed the positive "daisy age" strain; [[Boom Bap]] hardened the sound; G-funk and gangsta rap rose in parallel out West.

The era's end is conventionally marked by the deaths of Tupac Shakur (1996) and the Notorious B.I.G. (1997) — the tragic climax of [[East Coast vs West Coast]]. Part of the arc of [[Hip Hop]] from party music to dominant pop form.
