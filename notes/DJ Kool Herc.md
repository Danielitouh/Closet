# DJ Kool Herc

Clive Campbell, "the Father of Hip Hop." Emigrated from Jamaica at 12, absorbing the competitive sound-system culture and his father's record collection. As a Bronx teenager he noticed dancers were most energized during the short percussion interludes in funk and soul records — so he isolated and extended them.

His "merry-go-round" technique — cueing the break on one turntable while the other played, or looping one break with two copies of the same record — turned a six-second drum solo into an endless groove. That technique is the [[Breakbeat]], the seed of [[Sampling in Hip Hop]] and of breaking itself.

He played the party that became the [[Birth of Hip Hop]].
