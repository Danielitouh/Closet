# East Coast vs West Coast

The defining feud of 1990s [[Hip Hop]]. New York's hardcore scene (Wu-Tang's *Enter the Wu-Tang*, 1993, and the [[Boom Bap]] school) squared off against the West Coast's G-funk (Dr. Dre, Death Row Records).

What began partly as a media narrative — rivalry sold records, as Kool Moe Dee and LL Cool J had learned earlier — turned deadly real: Tupac Shakur was shot in New York in 1994, signed to Death Row, weaponized the feud on *All Eyez on Me* (1996), and was killed in Las Vegas that September. The Notorious B.I.G. was killed in Los Angeles six months later.

The two deaths are used as the end markers of the [[Golden Age of Hip Hop]] — and the power vacuum they left helped [[Southern Hip Hop]] take over.
