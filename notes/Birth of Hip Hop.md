# Birth of Hip Hop

August 11, 1973: Cindy Campbell threw a back-to-school fundraiser in the rec room of 1520 Sedgwick Avenue in the Bronx (25¢ for girls, 50¢ for boys) so she could buy new clothes. Her 18-year-old brother [[DJ Kool Herc]] DJed. That party is now commonly celebrated as the birth of [[Hip Hop]].

Everything that defines the culture appeared in embryo that night: Herc looping percussion breaks ([[Breakbeat]]), dancers going wild during them (the first b-boys and b-girls), and his friend Coke La Rock talking rhythmically over the beats — shout-outs and rhymes that evolved into rap ([[The Four Elements of Hip Hop]]).
