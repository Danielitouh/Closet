# Breakbeat

The "break" is the stripped-down drum-and-rhythm interlude in a funk or soul record — the moment the dance floor erupts. [[DJ Kool Herc]]'s insight was to play *only* the breaks, chained and looped across two turntables.

Breaks named the dancers (b-boys and b-girls, as in "break"), gave MCs a canvas to rhyme over, and became hip hop's raw material: early anthems like the Incredible Bongo Band's "Apache" were breakbeat records repurposed by DJs. When samplers arrived, looping breaks by hand became [[Sampling in Hip Hop]].

Root of both the sound ([[Boom Bap]]) and the dance element of [[The Four Elements of Hip Hop]].
