# Southern Hip Hop

The third coast. Houston's Geto Boys (with Scarface) were the first Southern act to break nationally in the early 1990s; Atlanta, Memphis, and New Orleans built label-driven local scenes. The turning point: Outkast winning Best New Rap Group at the 1995 Source Awards — booed by the New York crowd, answered by André 3000's "the South got something to say."

Regional inventions kept coming: Miami bass, Memphis's dark strain (ancestor of phonk), crunk (Lil Jon's party-only call-and-response, early 2000s), snap, and chopped-and-screwed in Houston.

While [[East Coast vs West Coast]] consumed the coasts, the South quietly built the infrastructure that made Atlanta hip hop's capital — and incubated [[Trap Music]], the sound that now dominates [[Hip Hop]].
