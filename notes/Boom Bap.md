# Boom Bap

The signature 1990s East Coast sound — named for its kick ("boom") and snare ("bap") pattern. Gritty, swung drums from SP-1200/MPC samplers, chopped soul and jazz loops pitched down, filtered basslines ([[Sampling in Hip Hop]]).

Masters of the form: DJ Premier (Gang Starr), Pete Rock, RZA (Wu-Tang Clan), Havoc (Mobb Deep). Two seconds of jazz piano over dusty drums — a Nas or Wu-Tang classic.

The dominant sound of the [[Golden Age of Hip Hop]] and the New York side of [[East Coast vs West Coast]]; the aesthetic opposite of the synthesized [[Trap Music]] that later displaced it. Descended directly from the [[Breakbeat]].
