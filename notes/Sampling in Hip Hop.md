# Sampling in Hip Hop

Sampling — reusing recorded sound as an instrument — started as a workaround for not having instruments and became the sound of modern music. Four eras:

1. **Block party era** — DJs looping [[Breakbeat]]s live on turntables ("Apache" as ur-text).
2. **Golden crate-digging era (90s)** — SP-1200 and MPC samplers; producers like DJ Premier, Pete Rock, and RZA chopping jazz and soul into [[Boom Bap]]; the deeper and dustier the record, the better.
3. **Obscure/chop era (2000s)** — J Dilla and Madlib flipping Turkish funk, Japanese jazz fusion, warped textures into new rhythms.
4. **Clearance and creation era (late 2000s→)** — lawsuits made vintage samples expensive, spawning royalty-free sample marketplaces; modern producers (Alchemist, Metro Boomin, JPEGMAFIA) mix crate digging with stem splitters and digital chops.

The spirit never changed: find the soul in the sound and make it your own. Also central to [[Trap Music]]'s synthetic descendants and the [[Golden Age of Hip Hop]] aesthetic.
